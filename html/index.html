Tío Simón

Simón Díaz

Musician, composer, poet, comedian and the uncle of all Venezuelans:

Simón Díaz was a venezuelan artist, who dedicated his life to promote and enhance the national folklore and musical traditions among venezuelans. He was recognized with several awards around the world. Winner of a Latin Grammy and the first musician to be given the highest recognition confered by the Venezuelan State - "Libertador's Order" "Great Cord"-.

Here is a timeline of Simón Díaz life:

  • 1928 - was born in a small town: Barbacoas, Guarico in Venezuela.
  • 1949 - he moved to Caracas. There, he enrolls at the "Escuela Superior de Musica" and receives his first formal music and piano lessons.
  • 1960 - became a comedian and an actor in various Venezuelan films.
  • 1970 - produced and hosted 12 different TV shows, all of them devoted to promote Venezuelan music. One of these shows; "Contesta por Tío Simón", was devoted to teaching popular culture to children. This children's show was on the air for 10 years, during which time Díaz's viewers began calling him "Tio Simón" or Uncle Simon.
  • 1980 - published his best-known tune, "Caballo Viejo", which has been covered by international artists, such as; Julio Iglesias, Placido Domingo, Gipsy Kings and others.
  • 2002 - During a two-month national strike, Diaz recorded a television spot in which he sang a short song asking Chavez (the President at the time) to listen to those who opposed him while reminding opposition leaders that the president still enjoyed popular support.
  • 2008 - was awarded by the Latin Recording Academy Trustees with a Latin Grammy.
  • 2014 - dies at the age of 85.
